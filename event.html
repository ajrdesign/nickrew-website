Accomidations

If you are going to be spending the weekend in Portland, we suggest staying at McMenamins Grand Lodge. You can’t go wrong staying here - settled in the middle of Oregon wine country, it’s a landmark and a Portland favorite. There are 3 pubs on the grounds, as well as 2 separate restaurants, a disc golf course, movie theater, AND spa (complete with a salt-mineral soaking pool). Every night there are various live music options around the grounds to choose from - guaranteed you will not get bored staying at The Grand Lodge. Also, nearby there is the Portland public transport that will take you straight into the heart of Portland.

Because all McMenamin’s locations have a history and are renovated historical landmarks they are not your typical hotel layout. These are European style guestrooms which means that the rooms are wonderful and comfortable, but share conveniently located common restrooms. Don’t fear - these are not the hostel rooms you stayed in while backpacking around Europe, these rooms are very private. But because of this unique-ness, rooms at the Grand Lodge can be booked for $45.00 - $75.00 a night. So if you know you’ll be attending from out of town, make sure to book your room ASAP! If you decide to book a room, and then it turns out you won’t be able to make it, don’t worry! The hotel has a 48 hour prior to arrival cancellation policy - any cancellations made before two days of your reservations will be refunded.

Andrew and Nicki, along with members of the wedding party, will be staying at The Grand Lodge Saturday and Sunday night.

If you are only in for the day, or want to stay closer to the wedding venue in Hillsboro, there are a few choices within walking distance of the venue. SpringHill Suites is less than 1 mile walking distance, and Larkspur Landing is 1.6 miles away.
